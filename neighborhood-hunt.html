Neighborhood Hunt

Bringing Regent Square together through a year-long "treasure hunt"-type service in which newer residents can discover the neighborhood and the accompanying stories of older residents.

Introduction

Our team (Course: Designing for Service; Carnegie Mellon University) was tasked to design a city-scale service for Regent Square in Pennsylvania - a neighborhood made up of four separate municipalities - Pittsburgh, Edgewood, Swissvale, and Wilkinsburg.

Timeline: 4/15/19 - 5/1/19 (2 weeks)

My Role

I worked with my team on all parts of the service design process. Specifically, I shared responsibility for observation, interviews, brainstorming and ideation, development of our service concept, prototyping, service blueprinting and value exchange, and presentation.

Research

Field Observations

We began by setting out and exploring Regent Square as a team. We sought out to better understand the people, businesses, events, and space of the city in order to get a glance at any potential for improvement through service design.

Through our observational excursion, we found a neighborhood with a quaint atmosphere - full of history, nature, and eclectic houses and businesses.

  • Lots of mom-and-pop businesses: culturally diverse restaurants, variety of stores, dentists, salons, etc.
  • Historical buildings and sites
  • Mix of young and old people
  • Lots of people walking dogs
  • Lots of cars - crossing the road was difficult
  • Found Frick Park - large historical park in Pittsburgh - as part of the neighborhood
  • Drainage problems in some streets

In order to get more insight into life in Regent Square, we interviewed three long term residents who discussed their experiences in the neighborhood through phone call.

Insights and Potential Directions

  • Bureaucratic/ money issues due to different municipalities: Regent Square is a neighborhood made up of parts of the municipalities of Pittsburgh, Edgewood, Swissvale, and Wilkinsburg.
  • Drainage Problems: Regent square has started experiencing a lot of drainage problems, and these have not been addressed.
  • Common usage of Frick Park: The park, playground, Wilkins School, and Edgewood Town Center are shared with the rest of Swissvale and Wilkinsburg.
  • Run Around The Square: Annual 5K event that residents value; runs through neighborhood streets and ends in Frick Park
  • Yearly yard sale: Large, publicized springtime community-wide yearly garage sale hosted by the RSCA.
  • Influx of new residents now residing with older residents: Regent Square is made up of many new residents, as well as those that have been there for decades
  • Block parties: Occuring often amongst the neighborhood; an excuse for residents to hang out and have fun
  • Lots of dog owners

Brainstorming/ Ideation

While we were limited in tackling drainage or bureaucratic problems, we saw possibility in better connecting residents to their city and to each other. We aimed to conceptualize a service that would bring together old and new residents - possibly by supporting and intersecting with their highly cherished annual events (i.e., yard sale, 5k, block parties), the history throughout the neighborhood, or its businesses.
