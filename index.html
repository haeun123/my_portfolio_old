Hi, I'm Holly - a recent graduate of Carnegie Mellon University. I recieved a Bachelors of Science in Psychology and minor in Human-Computer Interaction. Having studied Cognitive Science for part of my education, I have employed algorithmic thinking and research skills to be a robust and critical designer and researcher.

Feeding Frenzy

EDGE-framework guided and evaluated educational game teaching middle-high school students about food web ecology and trophic interactions, as well as human impact.

SHFIT App

We addressed the issues surrounding shift swapping and unstable scheduling in our design of a mobile app/ virtual space where gym employees can swap shifts, message each other, and schedule their week.

Adventure Run (documentation in progress)

A city-scale service designed from core to surface to bring together residents of Regent Square through multiple “adventure” hotspots in the neighborhood to experience older residents’ stories.

AHAVA (documentation in progress)

I worked with a team through contextual research, interviews and observations to develop solutions to improve the experience of residents and staff of the AHAVA Memory Care Residence.

Air Quality and Asthma in Cities: Data Visualization (documentation in progress)

I created a kinetic visualization of air quality and asthma triggers in the 5 best and 5 worst ranked cities to live in for asthma sufferers.

Pointing Bakeoff 1 (documentation in progress)

In Designing for Human Centered Software, I worked with a team of three to design and develop ways to improve speed and accuracy in a pointing task and ended up as a finalist.
